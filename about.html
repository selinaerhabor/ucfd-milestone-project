Main Members of The Kinks

The Kinks were an English Rock band formed in Muswell Hill in 1963 by Ray and Dave Davies. Formerly known as The Ravens when first formed in 1963, until later changing their name to The Kinks in 1964. Like most bands of their era, the Kinks began as an R&B/blues outfit. As teenagers, the brothers began playing skiffle and rock & roll. The brothers recruited a schoolmate called Peter Quaife to play with them. Quaife also played guitar, but he switched to bass.

Within four years, the band had become the most staunchly English of all their contemporaries, drawing heavily from British music hall and traditional pop, as well as incorporating elements of country, folk, and blues.

American record producer, Sheldon "Shel" Talmy under contract to Pye Records, helped the band land a contract with Pye in 1964. Before signing to the label, Mick Avory joined the band as their drummer. Other members including John Dalton and Jim Rodford covered bass after Pete Quaife left in 1969. John Gosling joined the band in 1970 as their keyboardist. From 1978, other members including Ian Gibbons covered the keyboardist role. Throughout their long and varied career, the core of the Kinks remained the Davies brothers.

Ray Davies

Raymond Douglas Davies was born on June 21, 1944 and has been part of The Kinks since 1963. He was the lead singer, chief songwriter, and rhythm guitarist in The Kinks. He later went on to publish his semi-fictional 1995 memoir, X-Ray and a second memoir, Americana, in 2013.

Dave Davies

David Russell Gordon Davies was born on 3 February, 1947 and founded The Kinks in 1963 with Pete Quaife. He was the lead guitarist, backing and occasional lead singer. Davies was solely responsible for the signature distorted power chord sound on the Kinks' first hit, "You Really Got Me".

Pete Quaife

31 December 1943 - 23 June 2010
Peter Alexander Greenlaw Kinnes founded The Kinks in 1963 with Dave Davies and was the original bass guitarist for The Kinks from 1963-1969.

Mick Avory

Michael Charles Avory born 15 February, 1944 and is best known as the longtime drummer and percussionist for The Kinks which he was a member of from 1964-1984.

Ian Gibbons

Ian Gibbons was born on 18 July, 1952 and he was the keyboardist for The Kinks from 1979-1989. In 2008 he joined The Kast Off Kinks, a line-up which includes some past members from The Kinks - Mick Avory, Jim Rodford, John Dalton and John Gosling.

Jim Rodford

7 July 1941 - 20 January 2018
James Walter Rodford played bass guitar for several British rock groups. He was the bass guitarist for The Kinks from 1978 until they disbanded in 1996.

Bob Henrit

Robert John "Bob" Henrit was born 2 May, 1944 and is an English drummer who has been a member of several musical groups. Henrit replaced the long-serving drummer Mick Avory in 1984 after he left and remained a member of The Kinks until they disbanded in 1996.

John Dalton

John Dalton was born on 21 May, 1943 and is a British bass guitar player. He was best known as a member of The Kinks from 1966 & 1969 to 1976, replacing original member Pete Quaife.

John Gosling

John Gosling was born on 6 February, 1948 and was the keyboardist for The Kinks from 1970 to 1978, making the band an official five-piece during this time. In the 1990s, John became a founding member of the Kast Off Kinks until retiring in 2008.
